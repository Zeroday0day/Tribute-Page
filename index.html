Mark Zuckerberg

The visionary who connected billions and reshaped digital communication.

On 17 February 2020, Věra Jourová, Vice-President of the European Commission in charge of Values and Transparency, received Mark Zuckerberg, CEO of Facebook.

Here's a timeline of Mark Zuckerberg:

  • 1984 - Born in White Plains, New York
  • 2002 - Enters Harvard University
  • 2003 - Develops various software projects and applications during high school, already demonstrating a strong propensity for technological innovation
  • 2004 - Launches "TheFacebook" from his dorm room at Harvard
  • 2004 - Drops out of Harvard to focus full-time on Facebook
  • 2007 - Becomes a billionaire at the age of 23
  • 2008 - Launches the Facebook Platform, opening the ecosystem to third-party developers to create integrated applications
  • 2009 - Introduces the "Like" button on Facebook, becoming a key element in user interaction
  • 2010 - Named Person of the Year by Time Magazine
  • 2012 - Facebook reaches 1 billion active users
  • 2012 - Facebook goes public (IPO) and, in the same year, acquires Instagram
  • 2014 - Facebook acquires WhatsApp, further strengthening its presence in the messaging sector
  • 2015 - His first daughter, Max, is born
  • 2018 - Mark Zuckerberg testifies before the U.S. Congress following the Cambridge Analytica scandal, addressing issues related to privacy and data management
  • 2021 - Facebook rebrands as Meta Platforms, marking the beginning of a new era focused on the metaverse and emerging technologies
